### Evolution Highlights

- **Batch 1:** The starting point. No wash, just salt and basic spices on 1.7kg.
- **Batch 2:** First wash introduced (141g total for 5.98kg). Salt reduced 20% from batch 1's heavy 15.88 g/kg. Note: "need 20% more wash next time." Forgot garam masala — added it before hanging as a save.
- **Batch 3:** Wash unchanged from batch 2 despite the "increase 20%" note (same 149g for 6.02kg). Added tandoori masala at 1/3 of spice weight and garam masala. Salt set at 10% below base.
- **Batch 4:** Wash properly scaled up (~197g for 7.98kg). Added cayenne pepper and ancho/chipotle/guajillo chilies for heat. Discovery: 1.6kg of meat wasn't covered by the dredge seasoning — need more volume.
- **Batch 5:** Ran short on several ingredients (worcestershire, tandoori masala, cayenne). Made creative substitutions. Key learnings locked in: **increase wash by 30%**, **increase seasoning volume by 40%**, finer grind needs more volume.

### Scaling Approach

**Scaled to:** 10kg.
**Then applied:** Batch 5's learnings (+30% wash, +40% seasoning volume).

### Wash — 10kg with +30% Increase

Starting from batch 4's per-kg ratios, scaled to 10kg, then bumped 30%:

| Ingredient | Batch 4 (g/kg) | At 10kg | +30% Final |
|------------|----------------|---------|------------|
| Worcestershire sauce | 12.46 | 124.6 | **162.0g** |
| Red wine vinegar | 3.48 | 34.8 | **45.2g** |
| Apple cider vinegar | 2.82 | 28.2 | **36.7g** |
| Condimento blanco | 3.32 | 33.2 | **43.2g** |
| Black malt vinegar | 2.66 | 26.6 | **34.6g** |
| **Total wash** | **24.74** | **247.4** | **321.7g** |

The +30% ensures adequate coverage even as the meat warms and the wash thins out (batch 5 learning about temperature affecting usability).

### Seasoning — 10kg with Two Options

The batch 5 note says to increase seasoning volume by 40% to ensure full coverage, especially with finer grinds. But salt is different from coverage spices — over-salting is hard to fix. Here are two columns so you can decide:

| Ingredient | Batch 4 (g/kg) | Conservative (salt flat, rest +40%) | Full +40% (everything) |
|------------|----------------|-------------------------------------|------------------------|
| Salt | 13.85 | **138.5g** | **193.9g** |
| Black pepper | 1.74 | **24.4g** | **24.4g** |
| Coriander | 5.93 | **83.0g** | **83.0g** |
| Star anise | 1.19 /kg | **~17 pods** | **~17 pods** |
| Tandoori masala | 13.46 | **188.4g** | **188.4g** |
| Garam masala | ~0.50 (batch 3 rate) | **8g** | **8g** |
| Cayenne pepper | 1.25 | **17.5g** | **17.5g** |

**Ryan's call on salt:** Batch 4 and 5 both used ~13.5–13.85 g/kg and that tasted right. The "increase by 40%" note was about seasoning *volume for coverage* (the dredge running out on 1.6kg in batch 4, finer grinds needing more). Salt doesn't need a volume bump for coverage — it dissolves. **Recommendation: go Conservative on salt (138.5g) and +40% on everything else.**

### Chilies

Based on batch 4's ratios scaled to 10kg with the +40% volume increase:

| Type | Batch 4 (/7.98kg) | Scaled to 10kg | +40% |
|------|-------------------|----------------|------|
| Chipotle | 2 | 3 | **4–5** |
| Guajillo | 2 | 3 | **4–5** |

## 4. Process Notes from Batch History

These are the accumulated hard-won lessons from five batches:

1. **Grind coriander coarsely and separately** — You want big coriander pieces in the final product. Grind it on its own so you can control the texture. (Batches 3, 4, 5)

2. **Work quickly — temperature matters** — As the meat warms up, the wash becomes less usable because fat and myoglobin mix in. Keep the meat cold and work in batches if needed. (Batch 5)

3. **Weigh each piece before placing in the fridge for dry brine** — The meat sits spiced in the fridge for 1–2 days, which is functionally a dry brine. Weighing beforehand gives you a proper starting weight for tracking drying progress. (Batch 5)

4. **Make enough dredge to cover ALL the meat** — In batch 4, 1.6kg ran out of seasoning. The +40% increase should prevent this, but keep an eye on it. Better to have leftover spice mix than uncovered meat.

5. **Finer grind = need more volume** — If you grind spices finer than usual, the same weight covers less surface area. The +40% accounts for this. (Batch 5)

6. **Average daily weight loss is ~4.21% of initial weight** — From the batch 2 analysis. Use this to estimate when pieces are ready. A 500g piece loses ~21g/day, so expect ~13–15 days for medium pieces.

7. **Expected yield is ~45% of net weight** — Based on batch 5 projections. A 10kg batch should yield roughly 4.5kg of finished biltong.

8. **Estimated pieces: 25–30** — Batch 5 had 21 pieces from 8.2kg. Scaling to 10kg and assuming similar cut sizes.

9. **Don't forget the garam masala this time** — It was forgotten in batch 2 and had to be added last minute. Small amount (8g) but worth having in the spice mix from the start. (Batch 2)
